Academical Interests

I'm an analyst who place the particular interests in the area of mathematical physics and applied mathematics. More specifically, I spend the amount of my energy on the topics in integrable systems (integrable PDEs) and random matrix theory. The application of asymptotic methods, special functions, PDE analysis as well as complex analytical technique is the central to this work.

Riemann-Hilbert (RH) problem is the main tool used by me to study physically meaningful quantities arising in the fields of integrable differential equations and random matrix theory, in particular their asymptotics associated to special functions and Painlev√© family equations.

Recent preprints as well as published works can be found on arXiv and MathSciNet as well as ORCID, see also the Publications on this personal webpage.

Integrable System

  • Exactly solvable solutions of the integrable PDEs with Lax pair.

  • The scattering theory and spectral analysis for the integrable PDEs.

  • The long-time asymptotics for the Cauchy problem of the integrable PDEs, including nonzero boundaries and step-like initial data.

  • The transition asymptotics for the integrable PDEs.

Random Matrices Theory

  • Integrable kernels and related Fredholm determinant theory.

  • Analysis of gap, distribution and correlations functions.
